Core Knowledge

The 10-Step Myers Deal Lifecycle

Every deal at Myers follows the same proven process. Learn these 10 steps and you'll always know exactly where a deal stands and what happens next.

1
Lead Comes In

A motivated seller contacts Myers through marketing, referrals, or agent prospecting (D4D, cold calls, etc.)

2
AA — Appointment with Seller

Acquisition Agent meets the seller at the property. Build rapport, assess the property, understand motivation.

3
Signed Listing Agreement

Seller signs the Myers listing agreement, giving us permission to market the property to our investor network.

4
Property Marketed

Property goes out to our investor database, Slack channels, and marketing campaigns.

5
SA Generates Investor Interest

Sales Agent fields investor inquiries, answers questions, and schedules the one-time showing.

6
One-Time Investor Walkthrough/Showing

All interested investors see the property at the same time. Transparent, fair, competitive process.

7
Seller Accepts Highest Bid

Bids are collected and the seller selects the highest and best offer.

8
$5K Deposit Collected Within 1 Hour

The winning investor submits a $5,000 earnest money deposit within 1 hour of acceptance. This is non-negotiable.

9
TREC Contract via DocuSign

The official Texas Real Estate Commission contract is sent and executed through DocuSign.

10
Title Company Closes

Title company handles the closing. We do a double close — we buy from the seller and sell to the investor same day.

Understanding GP (Gross Profit)

The GP is the difference between what we buy the property for and what the investor pays. This is the company's revenue on the deal — and your commission is calculated from it.

🔑 Key Concept

Double Close = We buy from seller, then sell to investor, same day. Two separate transactions through the title company.

Agents earn commission on the GP. The bigger the spread between buy price and sell price, the more everyone earns. That's why smart negotiations and strong investor relationships matter.
