Rental Analysis Fundamentals

Cap Rate

Cap Rate tells an investor the annual return based on the property's income — ignoring financing. It's the universal language of rental evaluation.

// Cap Rate Formula
Cap Rate = NOI ÷ Purchase Price × 100

// Example: NOI = $8,400 / Purchase = $120,000
Cap Rate = $8,400 ÷ $120,000 = 7.0%

Gross Yield

A quick screening metric — doesn't account for expenses, but useful for fast comparisons:

// Gross Yield Formula
Gross Yield = Annual Rent ÷ Purchase Price × 100

// Example: Rent = $1,200/mo / Purchase = $120,000
Gross Yield = $14,400 ÷ $120,000 = 12.0%

Net Operating Income (NOI)

NOI is the true performance number — what's left after all operating expenses:

NOI = Annual Rent − Operating Expenses

// Operating Expenses include:
🏛️
Property Taxes

Varies by county

🛡️
Insurance

Landlord policy

🔧
Maintenance

~5-10% of rent

🚪
Vacancy

~5-8% allowance

👤
Management

8-10% if PM used

Break-Even Analysis

Break-even tells the investor: "What's the minimum rent I need to cover all costs?" This is critical for risk assessment.

Break-Even Rent = (All Annual Costs) ÷ 12

// If total annual costs = $12,600
Break-Even = $12,600 ÷ 12 = $1,050/mo
// If market rent = $1,200/mo → $150/mo cash flow cushion ✅
