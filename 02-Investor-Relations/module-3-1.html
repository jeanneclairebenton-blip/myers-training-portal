Investor Sit-Down Framework

⏱ 10 min 🏆 Milestone: Sit-Down Ready

💰 Why This Matters

Every sit-down you conduct puts 1–3 deals in your pipeline within 30 days. This is your single most powerful investor acquisition tool — master the framework and you'll never wonder where your next deal is coming from.

📖 LEARN

The Sit-Down: Your Discovery Call

The sit-down is our version of a discovery call. It's rapport-building backed by pointed questions — a structured conversation designed to turn a stranger into a partner.

🎯 The 5 Goals of Every Sit-Down
  1. Review the Myers workflow — Show how we find, evaluate, and deliver deals
  2. Understand their preferences — Pain points, objectives, buy-box criteria
  3. Dig into their last project numbers — Real data reveals real opportunities
  4. Get buy-in — Use the "If I can, will you?" close
  5. Create a relationship — Not a transaction. A partnership.

What the Sit-Down Builds

1

Establishing Trust

You become a person, not a pitch. They see competence and reliability.

2

Understanding Needs

Their buy-box, deal velocity, and pain points become crystal clear.

3

Long-Term Relationships

One great sit-down creates a buyer who stays for years.

💡 The "If I Can, Will You?" Close This is not a hard close. It's a commitment micro-test. Example: "If I can consistently bring you properties in your buy-box at the right numbers, would you be open to working with us exclusively?" — Let them say yes to a reasonable ask.
🔨 DO

Build Your Sit-Down Agenda

You have a hypothetical meeting with a new investor who owns 3 rental properties and wants to scale. Write your sit-down agenda covering all 5 goals.

✍️ Your Sit-Down Agenda

Include: opening rapport, Myers workflow overview, discovery questions, numbers review, and your close. Allocate time for each section.

✅ PROVE

Submit Your Sit-Down Agenda

Your agenda should demonstrate you understand:

  • When and how to present the Myers workflow
  • Which discovery questions to ask and when
  • How to dig into their past project numbers
  • How to use the "If I can, will you?" close naturally
