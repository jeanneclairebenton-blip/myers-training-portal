Cap Rate Benchmarks by Market

Cap Rates vary by market and risk profile. Here's how to read them:

< 4%

Overpriced / Low return

4–6%

Appreciating markets

6–8%

Strong cash flow

8%+

High yield / Higher risk

⚡ Cap Rate Context A high cap rate doesn't always mean a better deal — it often signals higher risk (rougher neighborhood, older property). A low cap rate in a prime area may appreciate significantly. Help your investors understand this nuance.

Gross Yield vs Net Yield

Gross Yield

Formula: Annual Rent ÷ Purchase Price

Quick screening metric. Doesn't account for expenses. Useful for fast comparisons across properties.

Target: 10%+ for initial screening

Net Yield

Formula: NOI ÷ (Purchase Price + Repairs)

The real number. Accounts for all operating expenses. This is what the investor actually earns.

Target: 6%+ for solid cash flow

Absorption Rate

Absorption rate tells you how fast properties are selling in a market — critical for pricing strategy:

📊 Absorption Rate = Sold Homes ÷ Available Inventory

0–6 months of inventory 🔴 Seller's Market — Prices rising
6–7 months of inventory 🟡 Balanced Market — Stable prices
7+ months of inventory 🟢 Buyer's Market — Negotiate harder
💡 Why Absorption Rate Matters for Rentals In a buyer's market (high inventory), investors can negotiate better purchase prices — improving their cap rate. In a seller's market, rental demand is usually higher — which means higher rents. Factor this into your analysis.
