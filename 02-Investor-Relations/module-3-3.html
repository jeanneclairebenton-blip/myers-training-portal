👥 Module 3.3

New vs Seasoned Investors

⏱ 8 min 🏆 Milestone: Investor Profiler

💰 Why This Matters

Using the wrong approach with the wrong investor type kills the deal before it starts. A new investor needs education; a seasoned investor needs proof you know their world. Learn to read and adapt.

📖 LEARN

Two Types, Two Approaches

Every investor you meet falls on a spectrum. The two ends of that spectrum require fundamentally different strategies:

🌱 New Investor

You're the Authority

Strengths:

  • High engagement — willing to listen
  • Excited about learning
  • Open to your guidance and recommendations
  • Loyal once trust is built

Challenges:

  • Lots of hand-holding required
  • Liquidity concerns — may not have cash ready
  • Scheduling conflicts (still working W-2)
  • Emotional blocks — fear of first purchase
🎯 Your Tactics
  • Host casual training courses
  • Lead with education — lunch & learns
  • Offer property tours as learning opportunities
  • Refer lenders & contractors to fill their knowledge gaps
  • Be patient — they'll buy when ready
🏆 Seasoned Investor

They Believe They're the Authority

Strengths:

  • Know exactly what they want
  • Full-time — they NEED deals
  • Can close quickly
  • Higher volume potential

Challenges:

  • Very picky on numbers
  • Submit lower offers
  • Believe they know more than you
  • Will test your competence immediately
🎯 Your Tactics
  • Use LLC research BEFORE the call
  • Know their buy-box, lending, business model
  • Open with insight, not education
  • Have a high-impact conversation
  • Prove your value through data, not pitches
⚡ The Key Difference With new investors, you lead with education — you're the teacher. With seasoned investors, you lead with preparation — you're the resource. Both require trust, but the path to trust is completely different.
🔨 DO

Profile & Plan Your Approach

Read each investor profile below. Identify whether they're new or seasoned, then describe your approach.

👤 Investor Profile A

"I've been listening to BiggerPockets for 6 months and finally saved $40K. I want to buy my first rental property but I'm not sure where to start. I'm still working my day job as an accountant."

👤 Investor Profile B

"I've flipped 30+ homes in the last 4 years. I use two hard money lenders and have a GC on retainer. I'm looking for off-market deals in the 75227 and 75228 zips, ARV under $250K. Don't waste my time with bad numbers."

✅ PROVE

Scenario Quiz

For each investor description, pick the right approach:

1. An investor says: "I just closed on my first duplex last month. I'm excited but nervous about managing tenants."

2. An investor says: "What's your assignment fee? I typically run my own comps on PropStream and I need 70% of ARV minus repairs."

3. An investor says: "I have $100K in savings and my financial advisor said real estate is a good hedge. What do you recommend?"

4. An investor says: "I close 3-4 properties a month and I'm expanding into your market. Show me what you've got."
