The 6-Part Investor Finder System

This is the most powerful prospecting system at Myers. Each part feeds into the next, creating a complete pipeline from property to phone call.

PART 1A
MLS/Realist → By Property
Find cash buyers near a specific property. Search for recent sales within a radius — who bought nearby with cash or hard money?
🔧 MLS / Realist
PART 1B
MLS/Realist → By ZIP Code
Bulk investor search. Pull all cash/HML transactions in an entire ZIP code. Great for building area-specific lists at scale.
🔧 MLS / Realist
PART 2
TX Comptroller Lookup
Look up LLC registrations and find mailing addresses. If a property was bought by "XYZ Investments LLC," this tells you who's behind it.
🔧 TX Comptroller
PART 3
Forewarn Skip Trace
Free skip tracing through your MLS subscription. Get phone numbers, email addresses, and identity verification for any person.
🔧 Forewarn (MLS tool)
PART 4A
Corporation Wiki Research
Discover how many properties an LLC owns and what other entities the same person controls. Reveals the scale of an investor's portfolio.
🔧 Corp Wiki / OpenCorporates
PART 4B
County Records Cross-Reference
Cross-reference person ↔ LLC ↔ deal history. Verify their actual transaction history at the county level for complete due diligence.
🔧 County Clerk Records

THE WORKFLOW — How It All Connects

🔄 The Complete Investor Finder Workflow

📍 Find Property → 🔍 Search Nearby Cash Buyers → 🏢 Identify LLC Names → 📋 Look Up LLC Details → 📞 Skip Trace → ✅ Verify Deal History → 📱 CALL
The Power of This System: By the time you pick up the phone, you already know: who they are, what LLCs they own, how many deals they've done, what areas they buy in, and how they fund. You're not guessing — you're calling a verified, qualified investor.
💡 Pro Tip: Run Parts 1–3 first to build your initial list. Parts 4A and 4B are for qualifying — use them to decide who's worth a call and who to prioritize. An investor with 20 properties on Corp Wiki gets called before someone with 1.
