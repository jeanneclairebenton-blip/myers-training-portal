What Is a Property Tax Scrape?

Property tax records are public information that reveals who owns what, how they purchased it, and whether they paid cash. By filtering for specific criteria, you can identify investors who are actively buying — especially those paying all cash, which signals experienced, well-funded buyers.

Tax Scrape vs. HML Scrape

🏦 HML Scrape (Mod 2.5)
Finds investors who use hard money lenders. These are active flippers — they borrow short-term money to buy, rehab, and resell.
🏛️ Tax Scrape (This Module)
Finds cash buyers through tax records. These are often more experienced investors with deeper pockets — they don't need to borrow.
Best Together: Use both scrape methods. HML scrapes find active flippers. Tax scrapes find cash-heavy investors. Together, you get the complete picture of who's buying in your market.

Step-by-Step Process

1
Open MLS / Realist Tax Data
Navigate to the Realist property tax module within your MLS system. This is where you'll access county tax assessment records.
2
Set Your Search Criteria
Filter by: cash transactions (no mortgage recorded), recent sale date (last 90–180 days), target ZIP codes, and non-owner-occupied (mailing address differs from property address — a classic investor indicator).
3
Identify Cash Buyers
Look for transactions where no deed of trust / mortgage was recorded. These are cash purchases. Also flag properties owned by LLCs — these are almost always investors.
4
Extract Owner Information
Record: owner name (or LLC), mailing address, property address, purchase price, and sale date. Export to your spreadsheet.
5
Skip Trace for Contact Details
Use Forewarn or your preferred skip tracing tool to find phone numbers and emails. For LLCs, check the TX Comptroller site for registered agent info.
6
Add to Your Investor Database
Import contacts into Zoho. Tag them as "Tax Scrape" so you can track which prospecting channel produces the best results.
