TREC Contract Walkthrough

⏱ 10 min 🏅 Milestone: Contract Confident

💰 Why this matters: Contract errors kill deals and cost thousands — know this document inside out. One wrong paragraph can blow up a closing and your commission with it.

📖 Learn

TREC 1-4 Family Residential Contract

This is the Texas standard for residential real estate transactions. We use TREC contracts only — no custom contracts. This ties directly to P.O.E.T. (Module 4.3).

Key Paragraphs You Must Know

Paragraph 1

Parties

Names of buyer and seller. Must match exactly — verify against IDs. LLC name must match registered entity.

Paragraph 4

License Holder Disclosure

Disclose if any party is a licensed agent. We always disclose — transparency builds trust.

Paragraph 5

Earnest Money

Minimum $5,000. Deposited within the specified time. Non-refundable (refundable ONLY if title won't clear). Credited toward purchase price at closing.

Paragraph 6

Title Policy & Survey

Title company information. Who pays for what. Survey requirements. Review carefully with the title company.

Paragraph 7

Property Condition

We buy as-is. No inspection contingency. No appraisal contingency. This is a key selling point for our sellers.

Paragraph 11

Special Provisions

Any additional terms go here. Leaseback agreements, repair credits, seller contingencies. Read and write this section carefully.

Paragraph 21

Notices

Always include seller name, phone, and email. This is critical for communication during the closing process.

Paragraph 23

Option Period

10 days or less with a fee (per P.O.E.T.). Option money is separate from earnest money. Delivered within the specified timeframe.

💰 Earnest Money Rules

Minimum: $5,000
Refundable? Only if title won't clear — otherwise non-refundable.
Deposit: Credited toward purchase price at closing.
Timeline: Must be delivered per contract terms — no delays.

Common Mistakes New Agents Make

  • Misspelling names or using wrong entity names
  • Forgetting to include phone/email in Paragraph 21 Notices
  • Setting option period too long (must be ≤ 10 days)
  • Not specifying earnest money amount or delivery method
  • Using custom contract language instead of standard TREC
  • Leaving Special Provisions blank when additional terms were discussed
