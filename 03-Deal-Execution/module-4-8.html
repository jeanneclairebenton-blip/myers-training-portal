📋 Module 4.8

Setting Seller Expectations

⏱ 8 min 🏅 Milestone: Expectation Expert

💰 Why this matters: Properly managed seller expectations = smooth closings = repeat deals + referrals. A confused or anxious seller can blow up a deal even after the contract is signed.

📖 Learn

After Offer Accepted: Say vs. Don't Say

The words you choose after the offer is accepted are just as important as the ones that closed the deal.

✅ Do Say

  • "The title company will reach out to you within a few days."
  • "Funding typically takes 24-48 hours after signing."
  • "We'll keep you updated every step of the way."
  • "If you have questions, call me directly."
  • "The process usually takes [X] weeks from here."
  • "We buy as-is — you don't need to clean or repair anything."

❌ Don't Say

  • "We'll close tomorrow." (Unless you're certain)
  • "Don't worry about anything." (Too vague)
  • "This will be easy." (Oversimplifying)
  • "I'll have your money by Friday." (Without confirmation)
  • Anything about the investor's profit margin
  • Anything negative about their property
📖 Learn

Key Topics to Cover with Sellers

📅 Timeline Management

Set a realistic closing window. Explain each phase: title search, document preparation, signing, funding. Always pad your estimate — under promise, over deliver.

🏠 Leaseback Explanation

If the seller needs time to move out, explain the leaseback clearly: they can stay X days after closing, with a daily rate and a deposit. Push for a leaseback deposit — especially for hoarder houses — to ensure the property is cleared.

🏢 Title Company's Role

Explain that the title company is a neutral third party. They verify ownership, check for liens, prepare documents, and handle the money. The seller should expect to hear from them.

😰 Handling Seller Anxiety

Sellers get nervous — it's natural. Proactive communication prevents panic calls. Send updates even when there's nothing new: "Just checking in — everything is on track."

📝 Section 21 Notices — Non-Negotiable

Always include seller name, phone, and email in Section 21 NOTICES. This is how the title company and all parties communicate. Missing info = delays.

📖 Learn

CRM Notes — Required After Every Appointment

Update CRM immediately after the appointment while the info is fresh. Don't wait until you get back to the office.

  • ☐
    Detailed property condition — Roof, foundation, HVAC, plumbing, cosmetic issues, hoarder status
  • ☐
    Seller situation — Motivation, urgency, financial situation, family dynamics
  • ☐
    Timeline — When they need to close, move-out needs, leaseback requested?
  • ☐
    Offers discussed — Numbers mentioned, seller's expectations, competing offers
  • ☐
    Next steps — What you promised, what's pending, follow-up date
  • ☐
    Contact info verified — Name, phone, email confirmed for Section 21

⚡ Critical Rule

Post-appointment: Update CRM immediately while info is fresh. Details you forget today become deals you lose tomorrow. If you drive away without updating, you will forget something important.

🔨 Do

Write a Post-Acceptance Communication

Write a sample message to a seller after their offer has been accepted. Cover all the key topics.

  1. Write a text/email to a seller confirming offer acceptance.
  2. Include: timeline expectation, next steps, who will contact them, and your direct phone number.
  3. Write a follow-up check-in message for 3 days later.
  4. Write a "closing is scheduled" notification message.
✅ Prove

Submit Your Seller Communication Draft

Submit all 3 messages (acceptance, 3-day check-in, closing scheduled) to your Team Lead for review. They should be:

  • Professional but warm
  • Clear about timeline and expectations
  • Include all necessary details
  • Free of promises you can't guarantee
Submission: Share your 3 messages with your Team Lead. Be ready to explain your word choices.
