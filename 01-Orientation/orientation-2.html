Rules & Compliance

The non-negotiables that protect you, the buyer, and Myers.
Acquisition Discipline

See It Before You Buy It

Don't acquire a property you haven't seen — unless the numbers are significantly de-risked.

Never Post Below Cost

Deals must not be posted for less than the price they were acquired for.

Confidentiality & Verification

Verify Before You Disclose

Never share a property address until the investor is verified by signing the MVP agreement.

Keep It Off The Open Market

When pitching, advertising, or posting on social media, never reveal the address or the showing time. The address and showing time are confidential — protecting them protects the deal.

Marketing & TREC

Logo Placement

The Myers logo goes on all marketing materials where TREC requires it. Myers need not be the dominant brand on your materials.

Stay Compliant

Everything you publish must meet all TREC requirements. When in doubt on a disclosure or attribution, check before you post.

Reporting & Splits

Report Brokered & Wholesaled Deals

All transactions that are brokered or wholesaled in nature must be reported to Myers. Exception: No report needed when you're closing to rent, flip, or otherwise hold the home yourself.

When Commission Splits Apply

If you ever receive a commission for real estate services, you are responsible for all brokerage splits — including when you're a principal to the transaction. This includes: commissions from purchasing a listed property, commissions from helping another party buy or sell, and lease commissions.

Investor-first reward: Myers takes no broker fee on traditional brokerage referrals you pass to another agent.
