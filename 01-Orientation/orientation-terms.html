Key Terms & Glossary

The vocabulary you'll use every day.

Master these terms — you'll hear them in every meeting, every deal review, and every conversation with investors.

Absorption Rate

The rate at which available properties are sold in a market during a given time period. Used to determine market speed.

After Repair Value (ARV)

The estimated value of a property after all renovations are complete. The starting point for every deal analysis.

Cap Rate

Capitalization rate. Annual net operating income divided by property value. Used to evaluate rental investment returns.

Discount Rate

The percentage applied to ARV to calculate your maximum allowable offer. Myers typically uses 75%.

Flip

Buy a property, renovate it, and sell it for profit. One of the core investment strategies.

Frontside & Backside (FS / BS)

Frontside = revenue from the initial sale to an investor. Backside = revenue from the investor's eventual resale.

Gross Return

Total revenue before expenses. The top-line number on any deal.

Rip (Spread / Arbitrage)

The profit margin between your acquisition cost and sale price. The bigger the rip, the better the deal.

Self-Gen & Company-Gen

Self-gen = leads you find yourself. Company-gen = leads provided by Myers through marketing and systems.

Wholesale

Buying a property under contract and assigning or double-closing to another buyer without renovating.

Wholetail

Selling a property as-is (or with minor cosmetic work) on the MLS, typically at retail or near-retail price.

Hard Money

Short-term, asset-based loans from private lenders. Higher interest rates but fast closing. Standard for investment deals.

BRRR

Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat. A strategy for building a rental portfolio.

DSCR Loan

Debt Service Coverage Ratio loan. Qualifies based on the property's rental income, not the borrower's personal income.
